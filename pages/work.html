My Part-time Job as a Sales Rep at Telus

Why I started this job

I’m a huge fan of Cantonese drama. Cantonese is such a beautiful language and I love it. So, I thought, why not find a job in Richmond so I can immerse myself in Cantonese all the time? During the interview, the manager asked me if I was fluent in Cantonese. I didn’t speak a single word of Cantonese at that time, but still managed to pass the interview.

How’s the training process

The first few days working there I had no idea how to talk to customers at all. Actually, I never expected someone as introverted as myself would end up in a role that involves such extensive customer interaction. The training process involved a significant amount of learning, memorization, and developing soft skills, which really helped me improve as a more flexible and professional staff. After 2 months of intense practice, I finally successfully completed my probationary period and became a permanent part-time sales rep. I really appreciate my supervisors and colleagues who always encourage me, practice the art of conversation/sales strategies with me and teach me Cantonese!!!

My Amazing Colleagues

I met some interesting people over the course of this half a year. The sweetest Canto girl who studies Chinese medicine and always teaches me Cantonese sentence by sentence. The supervisor who speaks 5 languages and is too hard-working that she will “work herself to death”. The guy who calls himself a future data analyst and always role-plays with me to help me learn Cantonese. The rich guy who rents two rooms, one for himself and one for his dogs. The lady that nobody understands when she speaks in Teochew, and the girl who’s so determined to get into UBC…

My Cantonese is getting a lot better!

Learning Cantonese is the most enjoyable yet challenging part of my work. Considering my proficiency as a native Mandarin speaker, initially I thought Cantonese shouldn’t be difficult for me at all. The scripts and word order are similar, it’s just that the pronunciations are different, so it should be quick to adapt to Cantonese sounds. Soon it became evident that my assumptions are so incorrect, I shouldn’t undermine the time and effort it takes to master a new language. Talking to real people is not like watching TVB, I could not understand anything without subtitles, especially when customers talk fast in a non Canto/HongKong accent. It took me more than 2 months to start talking in Cantonese, though the customers are always like “it’s okay we speak English and Mandarin” after hearing my first sentence. Surprisingly, the greatest obstacle on my journey of learning Cantonese is that everyone speaks good English or Mandarin. When they hear my broken Cantonese, they opt for a language that we are both fluent in. That’s when I decided to put more effort outside my working hours. I watched every Youtube tutorial and Spotify podcast that I could find. I think in Cantonese, talk in Cantonese, and write in Cantonese till I can carry a full Cantonese conversation.

Despite Cantonese, the job did teach me something else:

A collaborative and supportive working environment is essential for personal and professional growth.

My colleagues are the best colleagues. They gave me opportunities and encouraged me to take on more responsibilities to build confidence. When confronting tough customers, they were always there to hold my back. My effort is valued when working with them and I feel safe to ask for help or guidance.

Not to take criticism personally.

Customer service is stressful. There are always customers who give me a hard time by pushing me to do what I’m not allowed to do or accusing me for what I didn’t do. When I don’t think their criticism is just, I respect their courage to say it out loud to my face and move on.

Appreciate my education.

Upon working here I realized that not everyone has the opportunity to receive higher education and gain the skills that would lead them to a more enhanced career. This makes me appreciate my university education which allows me to get access to resources, gain technical skills, and expand my network.
